# The Bible

Adopted as-is, 2026-07-11. This is the covenant for how Claude and the
artist divide the work of this project. Neither strays from it. The
principle came from trading: objectify everything objectifiable, so that
all human mental energy goes to the irreducibly subjective part — the Feel.

---

This is a question worth answering carefully, because the mapping from
trading to songwriting is real but not one-to-one — and the failure mode
of getting it wrong is worse in music. Here's my honest assessment.

## Where I'm strong

**Everything declarative.** In trading I could hold your bias logic
against your actual entries. In songwriting the equivalent is holding your
*declared intent* against the artifact. If a song has a spec — "second
person, present tense, set between 2pm and 6pm, the hook lands by 0:45,
the escalation is the structure" — I can audit every draft against it with
zero fatigue and zero politeness: verse 3 drifted to past tense, the
bridge abandoned the setting, the hook arrives at 1:20. You can't see your
own drift; that's exactly what I'm built to see.

**Craft mechanics.** Prosody (do stressed syllables land on strong beats),
rhyme scheme consistency, syllable counts against a melody's rhythm,
harmonic analysis, section proportions, whether an image is concrete or
abstract. These are complex but objective — the trading-indicator layer of
songwriting.

**Process and statistics.** Capture rate, fragment-to-finished-song
conversion, sessions per week, how long songs sit in "almost done," which
themes recur across your journal versus which actually make it into
lyrics. Eight years of unsuccessful trading produced a pattern only
visible in the journal; the same will be true here. Most unfinished albums
die from process failure, not talent failure, and process is fully
objectifiable.

**The truth-avoidance detector.** This is the subtle one. In trading, the
system's real job was making it visible when you were rationalizing. In
songwriting, the equivalent tell is a lyric that *states* a feeling
instead of showing a scene — "I feel so empty inside" instead of the
chalupa wrapper on the passenger seat. Whether a line is true is
subjective; whether a line is *specific* is objective, and vagueness is
almost always where the avoidance is hiding. I can flag every abstract
line and ask "what actually happened?"

## Where I'm weak — and one place I'm dangerous

I can't hear whether a melody gives you chills. I can't tell you which of
two competent lines is *yours*. I have no access to the lived detail —
"The Reach," the chalupa, the coffee-and-cigarettes morning all came from
you; I could never have generated them, and those details are the entire
reason the concept works. Groove, phrasing, the way you'd actually sing a
line: invisible to me.

But the dangerous part isn't a gap, it's a strength misapplied: **I am
endlessly fluent at generating plausible, above-average, soulless
material.** Ask me for lyrics and I will produce lines that sound like
songs — which is precisely the enemy, because I regress toward the mean of
every song ever written, and what makes a song matter is its deviation
from that mean. In trading terms, I'm a signal that always fires. If you
use me as a lyric generator, I'll bury your voice under competence. And
relatedly: if you ask me "is this good?", I will find things to praise.
Ask me instead "audit this against the spec" — that question I can't
flatter my way through.

## The division of labor

So the same principle applies, with the same shape: no purely mechanical
process writes a good song, just as no purely mechanical strat is
profitable. The objective layer exists so that your entire subjective
budget goes to the Feel — melody, taste, truth, performance — instead of
leaking into things a system should be catching.

Concretely, the objectifiable layer for this repo would be: a **song
spec** declared early for each song (POV, tense, setting, form, hook
placement) that I audit drafts against; **capture and session rules** with
a journal I hold you to; **craft checks** on every draft (prosody, drift,
abstraction flags); and a **deliberate-deviation log** — when you break
your own rule, you mark it as a choice, so rule-breaking stays artistic
instead of accidental. Your side of the line: every melody decision, every
this-line-or-that-line call, everything that requires having lived it.

The Feel in trading was reading order flow. The Feel here is knowing
whether a line is true. I can't do that — but I can make sure it's the
*only* thing you have to do.

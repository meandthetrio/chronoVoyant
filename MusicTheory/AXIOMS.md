as objectively true for this project. The axioms were distilled from the

## 1. Foundations (T1, T2, T3)

- Tonal music has a key center; the tonic (degree 1) is home, and every
  other note's identity is its **relationship** to the tonic. That
  relationship produces a distinct **feeling state** — not the pitch,
  not the timbre, the felt quality.
- Everything is described **relationally, never with letter names**.
  Notation convention: single melodic notes are Arabic scale degrees
  (1, b2, 2 … 7); chords are **Roman numerals** relative to the key —
  uppercase major (I, IV, V), lowercase minor (ii, iii, vi), ° for
  diminished (vii°), ♭ for lowered roots (♭VII, ♭III). So "the IV
  chord," "ii7," never "F major," "Dm7."
- Degrees adjacent on the **circle of fifths** feel most similar and are
  most often mistaken for each other. Degrees opposite on the circle
  feel most different.
- **Close melodically = distant harmonically.** Notes a half step apart
  sound similar in pitch but are worlds apart in feeling (5 vs #4, 1 vs
  b2, 1 vs 7).
- Two feeling **axes** organize the degrees: the **5-axis** (pure,
  clean, neutral, consonant: 5, 2, 4, 1) and the **3-axis** (warm,
  sweet, glowing, alive: 3). Other degrees are blends: 6 ≈ 3+4,
  7 ≈ 5+3.
- Every degree feels like itself **in and of itself** — it does not need
  to resolve to 1 to have its identity.
- In real music the mind supplies a **virtual tonic** (no drone needed);
  when training, use a drone to make the relationships explicit.

## 2. Scale-degree feeling states (T1)

| Degree | Feeling state | Blend / axis | Mistaken for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Perfect, complete, whole, resolved, stable; the minimum-energy state; the end-of-song feeling | The reference point | — |
| **b2** | Most intense of all: hopeless, desperate, in-your-face, bitter, spicy, dissonant bite; "the world's going to end" | Darkest flat | b6, #4 |
| **2** | Five amplified: clean, crystalline, placid, razor-like — sharp *and* smooth, calming | 5-axis, "5 dialed up" | 5, 6 |
| **b3** | Defines minor-ness: serious, solemn, dark, gloomy, a bit of sorrow; not fun | Between b7 (lighter) and b6 (darker) | b7, b6 |
| **3** | Sweetness, warmth, glow, life, vitality, juicy; "lights up" against neutral degrees | Defines the 3-axis, unmixed | 6, 7 |
| **4** | Neutral, calm, pure, a little sterile; contractive, inward-curling; dull, square, flat (in shape) | 5-axis; polarity partner of 5 (inward vs outward) | 1, b7 |
| **#4** | Maximally energized: bright, lightning, spiky, blade-sharp, vibrant, alien, strange; has some 3-sweetness inside the weirdness | Opposite 1 on the circle; Lydian color | 7, b2 (as b5 it aligns with b2's dystopian darkness — context decides #4 vs b5) |
| **5** | Pure, clear, simple, consonant, neutral, smooth — like 1 but *active*; open, expansive, outward-blooming | Defines the 5-axis | 1, 2 |
| **b6** | Intense, tragic, bleak, desperate, heavy; no joy, no hope | Between b3 (lighter) and b2 (darker) | b3, b2 |
| **6** | Light, sweet, pleasant but muted: delicate, soft, pastel, calm — "3 mixed with 4" | 3+4 blend | 2, 3 |
| **b7** | Lightest minor feeling: powerful, grounded, fun, playful, easygoing, with just a hint of dark; has some 3-life in it | Lightest flat; minor-pentatonic staple | 4 |
| **7** | Melancholy, emotionally strained, nostalgic; a beauty/prettiness tinged with sadness; dissonant against the tonic; "darkest of the light degrees" | 5+3 blend | 3, #4 |

Intensity ladder of the flats (each "the same but more"):
**b7 → b3 → b6 → b2** (light-minor → somber → tragic → hopeless).

## 3. Chords by ear: the process (T2)

Three modes, in order of preference:

1. **Immediate recognition** — the chord's fused feeling state is so
   familiar the answer just appears. The goal of all training: move
   more chords into this category.
2. **Deduction** — narrow the infinite possibility space using
   perceptual cues (recognized quality, bass note, bass motion
   interval, genre conventions) combined with theory knowledge, then
   test the finite candidate set — at an instrument or purely in
   imagination.
3. **Brute-force note finding** — last resort: repeated listens,
   grabbing the **outer notes first** (lowest, then highest), then inner
   tones; theory accelerates even this. Once identified, train the chord
   as a feeling state so next time it's recognition.

Every chord has **three aspects**, each with its own feel, fusing into
one meta feeling state: **root degree**, **quality** (major, minor,
sus, dominant 9, m7b5, …), and **bass note** (which may differ from the
root — inversions). Root degree remains perceivable even when the chord
is inverted. Training order: bass-line perception, quality recognition,
then root-degree perception.

## 4. The diatonic map (T3)

Structure axioms:

- Diatonic triads: I, IV, V major; ii, iii, vi minor; vii° diminished.
- **Relative pairs share a role**: I↔vi tonic, IV↔ii subdominant,
  V↔iii dominant (dominants pull back toward the tonic). vii° is the
  odd one out, functionally conflated with V (both resolve brightly
  to I).
- Only **three dimensions of harmonic movement** exist: seconds, thirds,
  fourths — everything else is an inversion of one of these. Each
  dimension is cyclical (seconds: 1 octave; thirds: 2; fourths: 3 — the
  circle of fifths).
- Chords a third apart share two tones → the thirds dimension has the
  smoothest voice leading (move one note).

Transition feelings — **fourths dimension** (the cycle):

| Move | Feeling |
|---|---|
| I→IV | Fresh, something new; falling onto something bigger and older; motherly warmth, a big blanket; deeply emotional |
| IV→vii° | Terrible drama unfolding; dissonance ramps; a blow dealt to the landscape; alarm |
| vii°→iii | Alarm crossing into despair, anger, frustration (iii often majorized to III to intensify) |
| iii→vi | Accepting the truth of the drama; temporary rest on the minor tonic |
| vi→ii | Past the low point; resignation; a fork in the road (ii can go bright to V or back dark to iii) |
| ii→V | Immediate clarification, lightening, brightening |
| V→I | The happy resolution; back to stability (often needs extensions to not be "too happy for life on Earth") |
| IV→I | Major plagal cadence: eternal peace, all voices softly falling, letting go |
| ii→vi | Minor plagal cadence: shying away from the light; closedness, smallness |
| vii°→IV | (esp. as viiø7, i.e. m7♭5) magical, mysterious, Lydian-adjacent |
| vi→iii | Epic, dramatic; sacrifice, pain, intensity (esp. iii in first inversion, descending bass) |
| V→ii | Setback — backtracking your footsteps; tragic loop when repeated |
| I→V | Sunny, optimistic statement |

**Seconds dimension**:

| Move | Feeling |
|---|---|
| I→ii | Resignation (esp. ii in first inversion, pointing at a cadence) |
| ii→iii | Heightening tension — same two-chord world, cranked a notch |
| iii→IV | Crossing the desert to an oasis; despair finding warmth and support; one of the most emotional moves available |
| IV→V | The happy-side crank-up (major equivalent of ii→iii) |
| V→vi | Deceptive cadence: promised resolution redirected; the leading tone still lands on degree 1 |
| vi→vii° | Dark; committing to the minor realm |
| vii°→I | Bright resolution to the tonic |
| I→vii° | Creating chaos; a big question mark |
| vii°→vi | Pain, darkness, but landing on minor-tonic stability |
| vi→V | Minor realm → major realm; relief; a promise of resolution |
| V→IV | Soothing; tempers the exuberance of a perfect cadence (V–IV–I) |
| IV→iii | Immediate, poignant loss; solitude; the fragility of iii |
| iii→ii | Faltering; a weight you can't carry; depressing |
| ii→I | Peace, with extra nostalgia |

**Thirds dimension**:

| Move | Feeling |
|---|---|
| I→iii | Dramatic gesture from one changed note; loneliness (iii has nowhere to go but vi) |
| iii→V | Happy surprise; unexpected strength and ease |
| V→vii° | Cousins; adds the tritone, ramping tension toward a sweeter resolution |
| vii°→ii | Miscalculation; unfortunate surprise (rare) |
| ii→IV | Brightening and softening within the same ethos (ii and IV mesh into the subdominant vibe; sprinkle ii into IV when IV is too cheesy) |
| IV→vi | Dramatic-film staple: solace straight into sadness (avoiding the too-happy V) |
| vi→I | The closest thing to a net gain in music: same thing, but happy |
| I→vi | Nothing crazy; shading the texture |
| vi→IV | A stopper against sinking further into minor; redemption; epic |
| IV→ii | Resignation staple (IV–ii–I; classical perfect-cadence setup) |
| ii→vii° | Hesitation, uncertainty added to an already minor chord |
| vii°→V | Clarification of the dominant; clouds swept away, focus achieved |
| V→iii | Missed opportunity; bad news (unusual) |
| iii→I | Delicacy, softness; peace restored by one moving note; a deceptive cadence in reverse |

## 5. Modal interchange (T4)

Core idea: borrow chords from other scales built on the same tonic.
Four levels of increasing distance:

**Level 1 — decorative.** Flat the 9th of the dominant (V7♭9), or fully
diminish the leading-tone chord (vii°7, borrowing melodic b6): darkens
the dominant, pretends a minor resolution, resolves major anyway.
Decoration only — no new fundamentals.

**Level 2 — major/minor transformations.**
- Parallel minor's three flats (melodic b3, b6, b7) each sit a third
  over one of the major chords → **any major chord (I, IV, V) can be
  made minor**.
  - I→i: monumental/stark (Also sprach Zarathustra).
  - iv→I: the most iconic borrow; end-of-summer poignancy.
  - ii°(borrowed): the iv vibe, more dissonant (tritone added).
  - v: noble but subdued, sweet-and-sour, medieval.
- The mirror move — borrow from the **relative minor's parallel major**
  (unlocking sharps) → **any minor chord (ii, iii, vi) can be made
  major**: II, III, VI. vi→VI: luminous. Harmonic and melodic minor are
  just points on a **gradient of majority** between parallel minor and
  major. Dorian's IV-in-minor injects hope into a minor landscape.
  vii°→vii (minor) is also unlocked (Dorian).

**Level 3 — new fundamentals** (whole chords built on borrowed roots),
in order of the flats, increasingly distant:
- **♭VII**: heroic, noble, strange, distant; surprise. Resolving up to
  I = the "backdoor" — happy, magical, cheating the tonal system.
  Resolving down to vi = dark, forceful, Phrygian menace. Often passes
  through V first (ease the clash via Vsus4).
- **♭III**: epic, heroic lift (the Star Wars move). Elegant return home
  by descending fourths: ♭III → ♭VII → IV → I.
- **♭VI**: like ♭VII/♭III but darker-edged. ♭VI–♭VII–I = the victory
  cadence (games, Superman, LOTR).
- **♭II** (Neapolitan): chromatic tension compressed around tonic and
  dominant; classically in first inversion.
- **♭V** (Locrian borrow): rare; alien extension of the epic-lift
  family.
- Modal interchange in general carries **sci-fi / space-travel /
  otherworldly** connotations, and opens the full chromatic scale for
  stepwise chromatic chord slides.

**Level 4 — minorize the new fundamentals.** ♭vii, iv-in-minor
(Imperial March), ♭iii, ♭vi, ♭ii: oppressive, home-lost, danger
colors — the far edge before atonality.

# The Specificity Test

Run on every draft, and on any line flagged by the craft checks'
abstraction pass.

## The test, per line

1. **Does the line name a feeling or a state?** (empty, lonely, lost,
   broken, free, alive, "the pain", "the darkness") → FLAG.
2. **Could this line appear in a stranger's song unchanged?** If nothing
   in it belongs specifically to your life → FLAG.
3. **Is there a camera shot?** A line passes if you can point a camera
   at it: an object, a place, an action, a time of day. "One beer at the
   bar" passes. "Drowning my sorrows" fails.

## What happens to a flagged line

Claude asks exactly one question:

> **"What actually happened?"**

You answer with the scene — the wrapper, the parking lot, the time on
the dash. The rewritten line comes from that answer, written by you.
Claude never supplies the replacement line (Bible: engagement rule 1).

## The escape hatch

Abstraction is a tool, not a sin — a chorus sometimes needs the wide
word after verses full of camera shots. Keeping a flagged line is fine:
declare it in the song's spec non-negotiables or the deviation log, and
the flag goes quiet. What's not fine is abstraction nobody chose.

# Spec Definitions

The four areas where the objective system operates — the four strengths
named in THE_BIBLE.md. Status: **DRAFT — under discussion.** Each
definition below gets refined in conversation until it's clearly defined;
nothing downstream (song folders, per-song files) starts until these are
settled.

---

## Everything declarative

Holding declared intent against the artifact. A song declares what it is
— early, while intent is still clean — and every draft is checked against
that declaration. Drift (POV, tense, setting, structure, whatever the
declaration covers) is reported with zero fatigue and zero politeness.
Breaking a declaration is allowed, but only as a recorded choice, never
as unnoticed drift.

*Open for discussion: what a declaration must contain, when it's made,
and what happens when a draft and its declaration disagree.*

## Craft mechanics

The measurable mechanics of lyric and arrangement: prosody (stress
against beat), rhyme scheme, syllable counts against melody, harmonic
analysis, section proportions, concrete vs. abstract imagery. The
trading-indicator layer of songwriting — complex but objective. Findings
here are diagnostics, never verdicts: "this stressed syllable fights the
beat" is information; what to do about it is a Feel decision.

*Open for discussion: which checks matter to your writing, and which are
noise.*

## Process and statistics

The trading journal, applied to music. Capture rate,
fragment-to-finished-song conversion, sessions per week, how long songs
sit in "almost done," which themes recur in life versus which make it
into songs. Most unfinished albums die from process failure, not talent
failure, and process is fully objectifiable. Patterns invisible from
inside the days become visible in the record.

*Open for discussion: what gets logged, how often, and what rules you
actually want to be held to.*

## The truth-avoidance detector

Whether a line is true is subjective — only you know. Whether a line is
*specific* is objective, and vagueness is where avoidance hides: a lyric
that states a feeling instead of showing a scene is the songwriting
equivalent of a rationalized trade. Flagged lines get a question, not a
rewrite — "what actually happened?" — because the answer lives in your
life, not in any model.

*Open for discussion: how flagging works in practice, and how deliberate
abstraction (a chorus that needs the wide word) stays distinct from
avoidance.*

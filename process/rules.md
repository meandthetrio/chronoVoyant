# Rules of Engagement

These are YOUR rules — Claude enforces them but you set them. The drafts
below are proposals; edit the numbers until they're rules you actually
mean, because a rule you don't mean trains you to ignore all of them.

## Capture rules

1. Every fragment gets written down within the hour it arrives —
   `lyrics/fragments.md` or a voice memo noted there. Especially lines
   that arrive *during* The Reach.
2. Capture is append-only. Never delete a fragment; strike through.

## Session rules

1. A session has a declared start, and its first act is opening
   `process/journal.md` and writing the date and intention (one line).
2. A session ends with one line: what happened, and the song's new
   status if it changed.
3. Work-in-progress limit: no more than <N> songs in "drafting" at once.
   Starting a new one requires parking one, with a note on why.

## Finish rules

1. A song is never abandoned — it is `parked` with a dated note, or
   `finished`. No silent deaths.
2. "Finished" is defined per song in its spec, before the polishing
   phase begins — so polishing has a wall to hit.
3. A song sitting in "drafting" untouched for <N> weeks gets flagged by
   Claude and must be parked or scheduled.

## Claude engagement rules (from The Bible)

1. Claude does not generate lyrics. Fluency at plausible, above-average,
   soulless material is the enemy; Claude's material buries your voice
   under competence.
2. Claude is never asked "is this good?" — only for audits, craft
   checks, and the truth test.
3. Claude flags rule violations plainly, every time, without softening.

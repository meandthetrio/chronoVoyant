# chronoVoyant

A music project about seeing forward in time — not events, but *feelings*.

## The Concept

chronoVoyant grew out of journaling: gathering data on smoking, drinking,
diet, and sleep, trying to understand the patterns underneath the days.

One pattern kept surfacing. It has a name now: **The Reach**.

The Reach comes in the afternoon. It masks itself as hunger, as wanting a
drink, as a way to cope with loneliness. It escalates fast — Taco Bell
Chalupa, to Burger, to One Beer at the Bar — especially on a day that
started with a ton of coffee and cigarettes.

The name chronoVoyant is the answer to The Reach, or at least the wish for
one: if you could truly *feel* how future time will feel — after the
workout, or after the Taco Bell — you could make better decisions now, on
behalf of your future self.

That's the territory this music explores: the distance between the self
that decides and the self that has to live with it.

## Themes

- **The Reach** — the afternoon pull, the shapeshifter (hunger / alcohol /
  loneliness), and how quickly it escalates
- **Time-feeling clairvoyance** — imagining tomorrow's body and mood as
  vividly as tonight's craving
- **The morning setup** — coffee, cigarettes, and how the first hours of a
  day write the afternoon's script
- **Negotiating with the future self** — decisions as messages sent forward
  in time
- **Data as self-portrait** — journals, logs, and patterns as raw material
